Product

CoordiFlow in one paragraph

CoordiFlow is a Uniswap v4 Hook on X Layer that turns token launches into behavior-aware coordination markets. Wallets are classified as Seeder, Builder, Stabilizer, or Restricted based on real pool interactions. Those personas affect fees, per-block access, reward eligibility, launch stages, and the coordination score, so the market matures through healthy participation instead of pure volume or first-block speed.

Good coordination should earn. Toxic flow should pay.
Launch defense

Anti-snipe, but bigger than anti-snipe

A normal anti-snipe hook protects the first launch moments. CoordiFlow protects the whole launch lifecycle. Early aggressive wallets can face tighter caps, higher effective fees, delayed reward eligibility, and Restricted classification. After entry, the hook keeps watching: wallets that seed liquidity, hold through volatility, and participate repeatedly can graduate into positive personas.

Anti-snipe

Early oversized or rapid buy pressure is slowed through persona-aware access and fee logic.

Anti-dump

Fast buy-sell round trips are counted, scored, and can push a wallet toward Restricted status.

Anti-whale

Large max trade size and outsized pool impact reduce score quality and can tighten access.

Pro-builder

Seeders, Builders, and Stabilizers earn better treatment because they improve market formation.

Tutorial

How to test the app

  1. Open the dashboard and connect a wallet funded with OKB for gas and USDT0 for testing.
  2. Confirm the wallet is on X Layer mainnet. The connect button and block counter should update from RPC.
  3. Go to Swap, choose USDT0 -> CFLOW, enter a small amount, approve USDT0, then swap.
  4. Flip the pair to CFLOW -> USDT0 and perform a small sell. The hook records behavior again.
  5. Open Personas and Activity. The wallet persona, score contribution, tx hashes, and hook-recorded actions should update from chain.
  6. Open Rewards Vault. Restricted or toxic behavior creates automatic penalty credits that can be inspected on-chain.
  7. Open Rehypothecation. Eligible wallets deposit idle CQUOTE, then the owner or agent deploys part of it to the strategy reserve and accrues yield.
  8. Open Persona Badges. Minted SBTs are on-chain proofs of wallet behavior, not images invented by the UI.

Integrations

Partner-native product stack

Flap-compatible launches

A Flap-style launch can create the token and initial market. CoordiFlow attaches as the v4 Hook layer, adding behavior scoring, dynamic fees, rewards, and market-stage logic. No private Flap API is required for the MVP.

X Layer Exchange OS signals

The live signal provider is an on-chain adapter for X Layer-native market intelligence. It can boost or flag wallet and market quality without replacing the hook's own behavior data.

Automatic penalty credits

Restricted flow is scored by the hook and credits the rewards system automatically. Production fee settlement routing is a future hardening item, but the penalty accounting is on-chain.

Agentic rehypothecation

Users deposit into a real vault. The strategy agent can deploy idle capital to the reserve, accrue yield, and route claimable OKB yield to eligible wallets. A fully automated keeper is planned.

Security model

What is protected in the MVP

  • Access control: external signals, strategy movement, and sensitive admin functions are owner or trusted-agent gated.
  • Reentrancy protection: vault-style user actions use explicit locks around deposit, deploy, claim, and reward movement.
  • Anti-griefing: wallet score impact is capped, per-wallet behavior is tracked, and Restricted personas face tighter access.
  • Fee safety: dynamic fee behavior stays bounded so the hook cannot return impossible or negative fee values.
  • Truth in integration: Aave-compatible reserve logic is live, but no unverified X Layer Aave pool is hardcoded.
